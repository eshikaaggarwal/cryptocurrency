Module 1: Evolution of Cryptocurrency

With the changing times, there has been a paradigm shift in the mode of transactions too. Begin with, there was Barter system, then came the Coins and Currencies and then Cryptocurrencies.
  1. Barter system had many disadvantages:
    • The needs of both parties needed to be overlapped
    • No intrinsic/declared value of goods that are to be bartered
    • Carrying goods from one place to another was difficult.
  2. Coins/currency system: Despite its many advantages in present day, there are multiple disadvantages as well:
    • Centralised regulation which is prone to manipulation.
    • Vulnerable to theft, hacking, etc.
    • Timing limitations/amount ceiling, etc. in transactions.

    To overcome these issues, in 2009 Bitcoins, a type of cryptocurrency came into picture.

  3. Cryptocurrency: Though a lot has been said against them, but here are some advantages on a positive note:
    • Almost impossible to hack the network.
    • As the ledger is distributed, thereâ€™s no scope for manipulation.
    • Foolproof with regards to theft, etc.
    • These are not time bound and there is no limit of transacting through them.

    After understanding the evolution, let us now study basics of cryptocurrency .

Cryptocurrency

Two main features of cryptocurrency will help understand its nature:
  • Digital money: No physical coins/currency.
  • Not pegged with any valuables: Cryptocurrency is standalone and not pegged with anything in real world. This also makes their value fluctuate erratically.

Module 2: Blockchain Technology and its use in Cryptocurrency

Credit lines: r.classen. Shutterstock.com

'Blockchain' is one of the two key technologies on which cryptocurrency works. Let us now dig deeper in blockchain to understand basics of it:
Components of Blockchain
P2P Network
  • Peer-to-peer network is decentralized and interconnected network of different users/operators /nodes.
  • Unlike traditional networks peers in block chain are not interconnected via central body, but are directly linked with each other.
  • All peers agree upon a common protocol regarding interaction with each other.
  • Each individual user in network is called node.
DLT
  • It is a distributed ledger technology.
  • Unlike traditional ledger, which is centrally maintained, DLT has all its records with all nodes in network. Any manipulation in ledger can be easily detected by other nodes by cross-referencing historical data.
  • DLT makes blockchain transparent and tamper-proof.
Hashing
  • It is basically a method of encryption of data in block.
  • It ensures that the data remains safe and can be accessed by only those having valid public and private key.
  • If A sends some message to B, then B can access it only by own private key and A's public key.

Blockchain Uses

Instead of using block chain out rightly for any project, feasibility of its use needs to be checked first. Following criteria for using block chain are:
  • When there is need to reduce intermediaries: Blockchain can help to smoothen out the process when multiple actors have to interact with each other.
  • When Digital assets are available: The area where blockchain is to be deployed need to have digital records, assets, e.g. Digital Aadhaar data, etc.
  • When interacting parties are in consensus: Domain where multiple parties interacting cannot come to consensus, are not conducive for the use of blockchain.

Cryptocurrency and blockchain are completely different and blockchain can have many potential uses. Personal Data Protection Bill may be revisited so that it can accommodate cross-border utilities of Distributed Ledger Technology.
IMC recommended draft bill- â€˜Banning of cryptocurrency and regulation of official Digital Currency Bill 2019â€™. It proposes criminal penalty on all activities related to cryptocurrency-mining, holding, selling, dealing, transfer etc.
The IMC's Report Proposes complete ban on private cryptocurrencies. The reasons for ban are as follows:
  • It lacks intrinsic value.
  • Prone to fluctuations.
  • Vulnerable to money-laundering.
  • Susceptible to fraud given the low level of digital literacy in the country.
RBI Circular Banning Cryptocurrency
  • Cryptocurrency is highly speculative demonstrated by its frequent and high fluctuation in value.
  • There is high risk of it being used in terror financing money-laundering.
  • Consumers are at high risk due to lack of knowledge of complex technologies underlying cryptos making consumers vulnerable.
  • Commercial banks, and therefore, economy may be seriously disrupted because of widespread use of cryptocurrency.
Supreme Court's decision on RBI's Circular
IAMAI (Internet and Mobile Association of India) challenged RBI's circular in supreme court on mainly two grounds:
  • RBI has no jurisdiction over trading activity
  • These assets are to be classified as commodities and not currency.
Supreme Court quashed RBI's circular and ruled in favour of IAMAI on following grounds:
  • Complete prohibition is excessive and disproportionate measure by RBI.
  • RBI did not prove factually the harm cryptos may pose to banks.
  • Crypto transactions do not operate in vacuum, but already many laws like PMLA, It Act, FEMA etc. are applicable.
  • Article 1g (1) (g) of constitution guarantees freedom to carry on trade. The ban by RBI violates this fundamental right of Indians and therefore the ban is unconstitutional.
Cryptocurrency and regulation of official Digital Currency Bill 2021
The bill proposes to ban cryptocurrency completely and intends to launch official digital currency regulated by RBI, i.e. CBDC (Central Bank Digital Currency).

Module 5: Way Forward

Credit Lines: LDWYTN. Shutterstock.com

There are different streams of opinions with respect to regulating cryptocurrency. While outright ban may not solve the problem, unregulated territory will also be dangerous.
  • Launching of CBDC (Central bank digital currency) will automatically encourage majority to opt for CBDC instead of private cryptos.
  • Outrightly criminalizing the dealing in cryptos may lead this market going under the carpet, and it will pose a serious threat.
  • Instead, stringent regulations on cryptos exchanges may help, e.g., these exchanges may be made reporting agencies under PMLA, just like banks.
  • Software, Internet and Fintech are Indiaâ€™s major fortress. Cryptos are mainly combination of these three. Therefore, better regulation can help India to benefit from cryptos.
  • The issue can be taken up on international forum, like BIS (Bank for International settlement), WTO, etc. Common global solution can help curb any illegal dealings using cryptos.
  • Coordinated efforts of different agencies â€“ RBI, SEBI, ED, IRDAI, IBA â€“ is very crucial in managing the new territory of cryptocurrency.

In conclusion, we can say that cryptocurrency certainly opens up new territory and potential opportunities in every field. India must not loose this opportunity and being a leader in IT and software, must seize it.

Therefore, rather than outright ban, we must think of accommodating this new currency so that it can aid and boost our economy.
